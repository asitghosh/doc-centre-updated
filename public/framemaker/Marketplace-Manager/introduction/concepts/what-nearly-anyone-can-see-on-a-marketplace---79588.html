What nearly anyone can see on a marketplace

NOTE: Some providers configure their marketplaces to hide content from unregistered users. When your preferred marketplace is one of these, you must register a user account, activate your new account, and then log in to it before you can see most of the features that topics in this guide describe.

Information in this section is sorted into two categories.

    • The landing page
    • Product profile pages

The landing page

Every AppDirect-powered marketplace has a page that loads first, by default, even for unregistered visitors. This is the landing page. It might function like a welcome screen (with a video tutorial, for example), link extensively to marketing material and white papers, or be a listing page that drops you directly into a shopping experience.

    • Product listings and their carousels
    • Product bundles
    • Marketplace FAQs
Product listings and their carousels

A clickable product list helps you to browse, find, and compare the products that matter most to you. Alternatively, you can render the product list as a paginated, multi-dimensional grid.

Listing pages support browsing the product catalog on a given marketplace. They expose the defined product categories and emphasize specific products that meet certain criteria.

Categories

Within the product catalog on a given marketplace, a provider can organize products together that serve a job function or business goal in common. For example, products within a “collaboration” category would often — but not inevitably — differ from products within a “marketing” category. However, a product that supports more than one of the purposes or goals that underlie a provider’s defined categories can easily belong to more than one category at a time. Categories help consumers to browse and find products quickly that are the most appropriate and useful to them.

Carousels

Carousels are a kind of promotional showcase in the marketplace shopping UI, which highlight one subset of products at a time.

    • Popular apps — The Popular Apps carousel draws your attention to the listed products that consumers buy most often.
    • Featured apps — The Featured Apps carousel draws your attention to products that marketplace providers choose explicitly to showcase.
    • App bundles — The App Bundles carousel draws your attention to a curated subset of configured bundles.

Marketplace FAQs

The administrators of an AppDirect-powered marketplace can post common questions and answer them in full view of all visitors.

Product profile pages

A product’s marketing profile might include and emphasize any of several kinds of information.

Table 1-2: Parts of a product profile

Features and benefits

Read a summary preview of the described product’s competitive benefits and features. Learn about key use cases and understand what sets a product apart from its competitors.

Featured customers

Learn which marquee customers the marketplace provider has specified that developers can cite, whose use of the described product serve as implicit testimonials. Developer claims of product effectiveness and high quality are bolstered when industry leaders prefer their products.

Reviews

On a marketplace that permits its consumers to write and post product reviews, the Reviews tab highlights opinions that they have shared about the described product.

NOTE: The marketplace provider sets policies that determine who can review a product and whether reviews are moderated before publication.

Ratings

Ratings from other real people help to shape purchasing decisions and encourage registered users to describe their own experiences. Anyone who writes a review can also submit a star rating to rank the product from 1 to 5. An averaged star rating is added to the profile page and appears next to the app in search results.

NOTE: The marketplace provider sets policies that determine who can rate a product and how many ratings must be submitted for a product before its ratings are averaged and the average is published.

Product Q&A forum

Consumers ask questions about the described product, and the product’s own developers answer these questions.

NOTE: The marketplace provider sets policies that determine whether product profile pages can include such forums.

Product support details

Read about the described product’s support options and minimum system requirements.

Product resources

External videos, articles, and web seminars can help consumers to consider a product in context.

Product editions and pricing

Consumers can learn about their options when looking to order an application. They can compare plans and pricing and see any special offers before placing an order.

Screenshots

Consumers can get a closer look at the product with screenshots that show the product in action.

Demo

See the product in action through a video or demo.
