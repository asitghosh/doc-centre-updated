Platform fundamentals

AppDirect is a unifying platform for the commercial services provided through physical and virtual servers in remote datacenters. It brings together the providers, developers, and consumers of a given service and connects their interrelated data, events, and transactions together for their mutual benefit. Through our platform, providers and developers at any scale can gain the power and flexibility to sell and provision essentially anything as a service ( XaaS) to essentially any business or consumer in the world. Common service models include software as a service ( SaaS), infrastructure as a service ( IaaS), and platform as a service ( PaaS).

Our deep catalog includes integrations with hundreds of the world’s leading SaaS products. Many of these are simple inbound integrations from vendors who consume AppDirect APIs easily. API consumption in more complex custom integrations, on the other hand, is bidirectional. Meanwhile, our modular approach to outbound integrations helps providers to use their preferred systems for identity and access management, customer relationship management, billing, taxation, and so on — unless they prefer to use ones that are standard to a default AppDirect deployment.

Likewise, we are locale-agnostic, supporting most of the world’s dominant languages and currencies — and business plan agnostic, supporting not only a wide array of revenue and pricing models, but even of payment methods, subscription contract stipulations, discounts, and promotions.

In addition to these many levels of customization, the look and feel for each marketplace instance conforms to its provider’s brand identity.

Desktop browser guidance

The companies that develop and distribute desktop browsers release updated versions of them continually, just as we develop and distribute updated versions of the AppDirect platform continually. Beyond this, your computer might use any of many possible operating system (OS) releases. The climate in which these systems and tools interoperate is as dynamic as it could possibly be. The number of possible permutations climbs every week.

So, while we pay close attention to making AppDirect work well in popular browsers on recent OS versions, only you can determine what browser and operating system are best for you. Understand Marketplace

Our UI portal called Marketplace is an environment to discover and buy products. It combines a searchable catalog of products and services for sale, a browsable storefront, and a transactional checkout service that initiates and provisions subscriptions. All registered users see and can use Marketplace.

Many AppDirect-powered storefronts allow even unregistered visitors to browse Marketplace product listings, although these figurative “window shoppers” must become registered users before they can actually buy anything in Marketplace or use anything in Application Manager.

Marketplace is part of every AppDirect plan and implementation.

Understand Application Manager

Application Manager is an environment for company administrators to manage their respective companies’ associated users, orders, contracts, and other details. Its features help them to:

    • Populate their company with its associated users.
    • Synchronize user credentials with their company’s Active Directory user base for identity and password federation.
    • Link to trusted resellers who are then authorized to buy products and services on behalf of the company’s associated users.
    • Assign product seats to some or all of the company’s associated users.
    • Assign specialist user roles within their company. These roles can address accounting and technical support duties.

App Manager is part of every AppDirect plan and implementation.

Understand Reseller Center

When your marketplace is configured to support direct engagement by resellers, a registered user who has the reseller role can:

    • See and use Reseller Center features generally in the user interface.
    • Register new companies on the provider’s marketplace and populate them with new users.
    • Accept reseller responsibilities over companies that he or she did not create, whose respective company administrator s link to him or her as a trusted reseller.
    • Search, filter, and view a list of all companies that he or she created or is linked to. Then, optionally, view fine-grained details about any of these companies, including its associated users and past orders.
    • Search, filter, and view a list of all user accounts that he or she created. Then, optionally, view fine-grained details about any of these users.
    • Make purchases on behalf of any user account that he or she created.
    • Search, filter, and view a list of all orders that he or she has ever placed on behalf of any customer. Then, optionally, view fine-grained details about any of these orders.
    • Search, filter, and view logs that itemize all of his or her reseller-related activity on the provider’s marketplace.
Understand Reseller Management Center

A provider can configure its marketplace to allow reseller access in general and, when desired, limit what users with the reseller role are empowered to do. In this context, Reseller Management Center is the UI portal from which someone with the reseller manager user role oversees registered resellers and all that they do on a given marketplace.

Reseller Management Center is an optional feature set in the Store plan. A registered user who has the reseller manager role can:

    • Register new companies in the reseller class and populate each of them with its first user, who always has both the reseller user role and the company administrator user role.
    • Search, filter, and view a list of all registered companies in the reseller class. Then, optionally, view fine-grained details about any of these companies, including its linked companies, associated users, associated orders, and reseller activities.
    • Search, filter, and view a list of all registered users who have the reseller user role. Then, optionally, view fine-grained details about any of those users’ reseller-related activities.
    • Search, filter, and view a list of all orders that are associated with the registered resellers on the provider’s marketplace — at either the product level or the product edition level. Then, optionally, view fine-grained details about any of these orders, including its history and activity.
    • Search, filter, and view a list of server log entries that describe such reseller-related events as:
      • COMPANY_ACTIVATED
      • COMPANY_CREATED
      • PAYMENT_MADE
      • RESELLER_LINKED
      • SUBSCRIPTION_CANCELLED
      • SUBSCRIPTION_PURCHASED
      • USER_ACTIVATED
      • USER_ASSIGNED
      • USER_INVITED
      • USER_JOINED
    • Click through to an external website where, optionally, the provider’s marketplace collects signup requests from prospective resellers.

Understand Marketplace Manager

Marketplace Manager is an environment to populate one AppDirect-powered marketplace with third-party products and services, configure its settings and policies, and administer essentially all aspects of its daily operation as a business.

Marketplace Manager includes the dashboards, reports, logs, analytics, and other tools to understand and shape the relationships between a given marketplace and its many constituents, including resellers, developers, and consumers.

Many AppDirect instances include Marketplace Manager, but it is not a baseline UI portal. Instead, access to it is restricted to the small subset of registered users who are authorized to see and use it, on the AppDirect plans that include it. The relevant user role is channel administrator.

Understand Network Manager

Part of the flexibility of the AppDirect platform is that it allows for — but never requires — a defined hierarchy of parent (corporate) and child (division or affiliate) marketplaces. When multiple AppDirect instances are all subordinate to central oversight by a corporate parent, our UI portal called Network Manager is where this oversight occurs.

Network Manager is the environment where corporate executives can oversee, override, and overrule what their channel administrators do on any affiliated, child marketplace.

The only AppDirect instances that include Network Manager are ones in the Network plan, and access to the Network Manager portal is restricted to the small subset of registered users who are authorized to see and use it. The relevant user role is corporate administrator.

Understand Developer Center

Developer Center is where the developers who work for third-party vendors describe, integrate, manage, and support the products that they sell through a given marketplace. Such vendors can build marketing profiles, configure pricing, review payments, orders and invoices, and manage customers.

Many AppDirect instances include Developer Center and many do not. Access to it is restricted to the small subset of registered users who are authorized to see and use it, on the AppDirect platform tiers and instances that include it. The relevant user role is developer.
