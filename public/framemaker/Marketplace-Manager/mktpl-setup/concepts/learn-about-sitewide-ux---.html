Understand options to update marketplace UX

As a channel administrator , you can refine and adjust multiple aspects of user experience on your marketplace.

NOTE: The controls to make such adjustments are distributed across multiple UI pages.

    • User experience changes to casual discovery of your marketplace through search engines
      • Edit the robots.txt file, which determines what parts of your marketplace an external search engine can index.
      • Edit the <META NAME="DESCRIPTION"> text that search engines quote in search result listings. In most cases, this descriptive snippet contains roughly 160 characters.
      • Edit the <META NAME="KEYWORDS"> text that search engines use in determining the sorting order of search results.
    • User experience changes from business strategy adjustments
      • Allow or forbid any involvement of third-party resellers — whose possible involvement affects user experience across multiple areas. Then, in cases when you allow reseller involvement:
        • Enable or disable the “sales opportunity” workflow for resellers.
        • Enable or disable the “lead” workflow for resellers.
    • User experience changes to new user acquisition
      • Include or exclude a mandatory CAPTCHA during registration.
      • Enforce or forgive telephone number collection during registration.
      • Expose or hide product assignment UI elements from company administrators when they invite potential new users to join their company on your marketplace.
      • Enable or disable delivery of an email notification message to newly registered users when a sales agent completed the registration workflow on their behalf.
      • Add custom fields to collect nonstandard information.
    • User experience changes to the login flow
      • Enable or disable a sitewide password, whose submission is prerequisite to the transmission of any UI rendering details — including the information to render even a standard login prompt — to client browsers.
      • Configure a user-level password policy — whose relative simplicity or intricacy tunes the balance between user convenience and user security.
    • User experience changes to finding and buying products
      • Enable or disable various payment methods.
      • Enable or disable various product types.
      • Edit the landing page layout.
      • Edit product categories.
      • Enable or disable edition ranking.
      • Populate product carousels; choose which of them to show; set where to show them.
      • Show or hide all consumer questions on product marketing profile pages.
      • Show or hide all technical support content on product marketing profile pages.
      • Allow or forbid assisted sales.
        • Allow or forbid sales agents to enter payment details on a consumer’s behalf during assisted sales.
        • Allow or forbid sales agents to cancel subscriptions on a user’s behalf.
        • Enforce universally, forgive universally, or leave to a sales agent’s own discretion whether to obtain consumer approval explicitly for each assisted sale.
        • Enable or disable automatic copying of billing-related email notification messages to an assisted sale’s associated sales agent.
    • User experience changes to Application Manager
      • Expose or hide company directory features, including company and user profiles.
      • Expose or hide the features to import assigned products from other environments.
    • User experience changes to Developer Center
      • Hide their own customer information from vendors.
      • Expose or hide a visibility toggle for each product on its respective management console.
    • Other user experience changes
      • Allow or forbid sales agents and technical support representatives to impersonate any registered user, temporarily, after receiving the user’s explicit and logged authorization to proceed.
      • Hide from consumers all explicit contact details for members of the provider company (the channel partner).
      • Add custom form fields and turn their visibility On or Off.

Understand storefront layout options

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Settings | General Settings > [ Edit homepage layout ]

You can design the look and feel of your storefront, using drag and drop panels to reorder its landing page, edit its settings, and add or remove elements — including promoted product carousels and categorical product sliders.
