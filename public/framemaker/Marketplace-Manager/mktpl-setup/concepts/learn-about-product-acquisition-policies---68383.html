Understand overriding vendor-set payment policies per edition

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Billing Settings | Billing Functionality

You can show or hide UI elements that help you to override two vendor-specified billing policies. Your authority to override these policies is not global. Instead, you can override them one at a time, per product edition, in much the same way that vendors define them.

    • One of these policies controls whether customers must enter a payment method while ordering the specified product edition.
    • The other policy determines whether all free-trial editions are upgraded automatically to paid editions when their respective trial periods end.

Understand ordering authority policies

Why to restrict who can place orders

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Billing Settings | Billing Functionality

You can set a policy that limits purchasing authority to two user roles exclusively. These are the company administrator role and the billing administrator role. When this policy is active, all users who lack even one of these role assignments must request that an administrator order products on their behalf and assign seats to them. This policy may lower the administrative overhead that vendors and company administrators face when every registered user is able to place orders.

Why to collect billing details for free-trial products

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Billing Settings | Billing Functionality

You can turn a marketplace behavior On or Off that collects billing details when consumers place orders for free-trial product editions. When the behavior is turned Off, the checkout process is simplified for free-trial product editions.

Why to collect billing details for non-billable products

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Billing Settings | Billing Functionality

You can turn a marketplace behavior On or Off that collects billing details when consumers order anything, including no-cost editions, free trial editions, and fully discounted products. When the behavior is turned Off, the checkout process is simplified for any order without charges.

Related topics

    • Set a billing details collection enforcement policy for all billable and non-billable products
Why to collect billing details for fully discounted products

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Billing Settings | Billing Functionality

You can turn a marketplace behavior On or Off that collects billing details when consumers apply a 100% discount toward their order of any product whose contract has a defined end-date. When the behavior is turned Off, the checkout process is simplified for any fully discounted order that has a defined end-date.

TIP: We never collect billing details when someone applies a 100% discount to the order of any product that lacks a defined end-date. For context, we often call these “one-time orders.”

One-click access can apply to no-cost products

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Billing Settings | Billing Functionality

You can turn a marketplace behavior On or Off that eliminates the purchase flow for free products and free-trial product editions. When the behavior is turned Off, all orders enforce checkout.

NOTE: One-click access is mutually exclusive to a setting that causes the automatic upgrade of expired free-trial product editions to billed product editions. You cannot turn one setting On without turning the other Off.

TIP: A one-click product does not collect any billing details during checkout.

Vendor clickwrap prompts can apply to one-click products

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Billing Settings | Billing Functionality

You can turn a marketplace behavior On or Off that empowers product vendors to enforce consumer agreement to terms and conditions “clickwrap” before they can obtain any one-click product.

TIP: A one-click product does not collect any billing details during checkout.
