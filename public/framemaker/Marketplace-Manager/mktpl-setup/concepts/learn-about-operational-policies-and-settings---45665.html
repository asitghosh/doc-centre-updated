Understand stealth mode

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Settings | Marketplace Functionality > Marketplace Password | Password Protected Marketplace

You can configure your marketplace to deny all requests from anyone who does not provide valid sitewide credentials when prompted. This feature restricts access while you configure and test marketplace settings.

Understand OpenID trusted realm editing

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Settings | Marketplace Functionality > Security | Add an OpenID Trusted Realm

You can configure OpenID authorization to apply implicitly and automatically to incoming requests from trusted realms. Your marketplace server then becomes an OpenID identity provider (IdP) to requests from white-listed URLs.

Understand payment gateway editing

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Billing Settings | Billing Functionality > Billing Settings | Payment Gateway

When you contracted with a financial services firm to authorize and process all incoming payments for your marketplace, that firm issued text strings to you, which your marketplace must return exactly to the firm’s payment gateway during each transaction.

AppDirect has already configured your marketplace to return these mandatory values correctly. However, you can edit them if they change for any reason.

CAUTION: Do not change payment gateway entries frivolously. Doing so can cause billing operations to fail. This, in turn, can lead vendors to suspend user access to their products. Such disruptions can jeopardize your organization’s reputation, partnerships, and revenue.

Supported payment gateways include Authorize.net , Beanstream , BlueSnap , PayPal Payflow Pro , and Worldpay. See https://docs.appdirect.com/developer/billing/third-party-integrations/paym,ent gateways.

Understand the importance of password policy

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Settings | Marketplace Functionality > Security | Password Policy

User account passwords help to protect your customers against data theft, social engineering, and financial fraud, while also helping to preserve your own organization’s reputation as a provider of trustworthy services.

You must choose and apply a password security policy from among three predefined options. These alternatives differ in their rules for password length and complexity.

TIP: We recommend that you always choose and apply the most secure policy. We recommend this because strong passwords are objectively better than weak ones at doing what a password is meant to do. Strong passwords are more effective than weak ones at preventing fraud and theft. Strong passwords are more effective than weak ones at protecting intangible assets, including your organization’s reputation and brand equity. Also, strong passwords are less vulnerable than weak ones to social engineering, dictionary attacks, and brute force attacks.

Understand the sitewide default user role setting

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Settings | Marketplace Functionality > Customer Roles | User’s Default Role

You can set which user role is preselected on the page where company administrators choose a user role to grant by default to their respective companies’ associated users.

Although you set which option is preselected for company administrators, each one of them is free to choose a different option than one that you set to be their default. Furthermore, they are free to assign different roles to their users than whichever role is their respective companies’ default.
