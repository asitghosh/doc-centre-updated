Understand the benefits of a well considered authority policy

In an abstract sense, each assignable user role begins as a range of thresholds: Such-and-such user has at least enough authority to do X, but possibly enough authority to do Y in addition to X.

Having made the decision that a certain user role should be enabled at all on your marketplace, your next task is to determine the scope of its authority. For example, is the role’s recipient authorized to see X, or see and rename it, or see and rename and delete it?

As you toggle aspects of authority On or Off for a certain user role, you determine how the role’s recipients fit into your marketplace and its community of authorized users.

Understand authority policies for assisted sales

NOTE: A “sales agent” is any registered user who completes assisted sales on behalf of other users. Only two user roles have authority to perform assisted sales. They are resellers and sales support representatives.

Topics in this section cover the following subjects.

    • Why to enable sales agent authority to bypass customer approval
    • Why to enable sales agent authority to enter payment details
Why to enable sales agent authority to bypass customer approval

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Settings | Marketplace Functionality

You can limit sales agent authority to conduct assisted sales without a target user’s explicit consent. There are three mutually exclusive alternatives.

    • Sales agents can complete any assisted sale without a target user’s consent or involvement.

      OR
    • Sales agents must obtain explicit consent from the target user, who completes each order.

      OR
    • Sales agents have personal discretion to choose on a case-by-case basis whether it is appropriate to obtain a target user’s consent.

NOTE: In relation to assisted sales, the “sales agent” designation can mean someone with the reseller user role, when your marketplace uses the reseller service, or someone with the sales support user role.

Why to enable sales agent authority to enter payment details

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Settings | Marketplace Functionality

You can turn a marketplace behavior On or Off that authorizes sales agents to enter a target user’s billing details during an assisted sale.

NOTE: In relation to assisted sales, the “sales agent” designation can mean someone with the reseller user role, when your marketplace uses the reseller service, or someone with the sales support user role.

Why let resellers join linked companies?

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Settings | Roles

You can turn a marketplace behavior On or Off that authorizes resellers to join any of their linked companies. After a reseller joins one of his or her linked companies, the company name is added to his or her company context list in the main navigation bar, immediately to the left of the Manage list. Later, upon choosing a given company from the company context list, the reseller can see, troubleshoot, and debug the company setup.

Related topics

    • Configure policies to constrain resellers
Why let resellers disable linked companies?

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Settings | Roles

You can turn a marketplace behavior On or Off that authorizes resellers to disable every user account that is associated with any of the reseller’s linked companies. Your choice here should be consistent with your vision for reseller operations on your marketplace. Should resellers merely drive product sales, for example, or do you want them to manage their linked companies?

Navigation path

You can turn a marketplace behavior On or Off that authorizes resellers to save incomplete orders as sales quotes, which can then be converted back into orders at any time for ease of completion.

You can turn a marketplace behavior On or Off that authorizes sales support representatives to view and resolve product provisioning events. Your choice here should be consistent with your vision for sales support representatives on your marketplace. Why let developers access customer data?

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Settings | Vendors

You can turn a marketplace behavior On or Off to suppress the display of customer records in Developer Center, which developers would otherwise see, regarding the registered users who have ordered any of their products or been assigned to their allocated seats.

Navigation path

You can turn a marketplace behavior On or Off that authorizes developers to manage any sales leads that their own product marketing profile pages generate.

Channel administrators , including you, can manage these same sales leads even when developers cannot.

Why let developers rank product editions?

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Billing Settings | Billing Functionality

You can turn a marketplace behavior On or Off that authorizes developers to use edition-ranking features, which are the basis for determining which subscription changes are upgrades and which are downgrades. When this behavior is turned Off, edition pricing is the sole criterion that ranks one edition over or under another.
