Billing feature aggregation and navigation

Table 6-14 is a quick reference to all finance-related features of Marketplace Manager, exposing which of these functions we cluster together and citing exactly where in the UI you can find each function.

Table 6-14: Finance feature navigation in Marketplace Manager


Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Billing |

Orders

Use UI elements and features here, optionally, to do any or all of the following.

    • Work with records of the products that marketplace customers have ordered, without regard to particular product editions.
      • Browse, filter (by any combination of order status, cost, and date), sort, and search the list of customer-ordered products, and export any or all of its underlying records to CSV.
      • View the highly detailed information in any product order that the list cites. Then:
        • Use options on the Order History tab to review each of a given order’s state transitions throughout its life cycle.
        • Use options on the Update Subscription tab to switch from one product edition to another when subscription contract stipulations allow it, change the number of consumable units when a product’s revenue model supports per-unit charges, or apply a discount.
        • Use options on the Activity tab to browse through all order-specific activities by the ordering company’s associated users. For example, the activity records tell you exactly when and which associated users created the order, requested product access, were assigned to the product, had their assignments revoked, modified the subscription contract, and so on.
        • Use options on the Users tab to browse all of the ordering company’s associated users who have been assigned to the described product during a given order’s active life.
    • Work narrowly with records of specific product editions that marketplace customers have ordered.
      • Browse, filter (by any combination of order status, cost, and date), sort, and search the list of customer-ordered product editions, and export any or all of its underlying records to CSV.
      • View the highly detailed information in any product edition order that the list cites. Then:
        • Add a reseller to the described order.
        • Edit or cancel the subscription contract that the described order created.

Invoices

Use UI elements and features here, optionally, to do any or all of the following.

    • Browse, filter (by status, by date, or both), sort, and search the list of issued invoices, and export any or all of its underlying records to CSV.
    • View the highly detailed information in any invoice that the list cites. Then:
      • Export an invoice to PDF.
      • Click through to the details of any payment made toward the balance of an invoice.

Payments

Use UI elements and features here, optionally, to do any or all of the following.

    • Browse, filter (by payment method, transaction type [meaning, payment or refund], payment result, payment date, or any combination of these), sort, and search the list of payments received, and export any or all underlying records to CSV.
    • View the highly detailed information in any payment record that the list cites.
      • Click through any invoice ID to load details about all payments made toward the cited invoice and, optionally, obtain a PDF summary of it.
      • Confirm manually — in the case of a payment whose processing failed (without the payment method being declined) — that your marketplace received the described payment even though there is no record of its processing.
      • Process a refund — in the case of a payment whose processing was completed successfully — through your payment gateway or record a refund manually for the described payment.

Quotes

Use UI elements and features here, optionally, to do any or all of the following.

    • Browse, filter (by quote date), sort, and search the list of all quotes that sales agents saved during their assisted sales sessions, and export any or all underlying records to CSV.
    • View the highly detailed information in any quote that the list cites.
      • Edit the quote, as long as its customer hasn’t already completed the corresponding purchase.
      • Delete the quote.
      • Check which sales agent saved the quote.
      • Check which customer received the quote.
      • Check the quote date.
      • Check which product and product edition the quote describes.

Reconciliation

Use UI elements and features here, optionally, to do any or all of the following.

    • In the Reconciliation table , browse, filter (by transaction date), sort, and search the list of all reconciliation transactions, record the payout for any product whose generated revenue is due for reconciliation, and export any or all of the table’s underlying records to CSV.
    • In the Payouts table , browse, filter (by payout status, payout date, or the combination of these), sort, and search the list of all payout transactions, and export any or all underlying records to CSV.
    • In the Revenue Shares table, browse, filter (by revenue-generation event dates), sort, and search the list of all revenue-share definition records, and export any or all underlying records to CSV.
      • View the highly detailed information in any revenue sharing record that the list cites.
        • Check which product a revenue-sharing agreement covers.
        • Check what revenue-sharing percentages are in effect today.
        • Check if the revenue-sharing percentages have ever been edited, and when, and to what effect, and by whom.
        • Export the product’s entire revenue-sharing history to CSV.


Manage > Channel > Settings > Billing Settings |

Billing Functionality

Use UI elements and features here, optionally, to do any or all of the following:

    • Purchase Restrictions — Set a daily purchasing limit on all member companies. Restrict all purchasing to just users who have either the company administrator role or the billing administrator role. (When purchasing is restricted by user role, end-users who lack either role assignment can only request that products be purchased on their behalf.)
    • Subscriptions — Set a grace period for past-due payments, before their delinquency triggers suspension of each associated subscription. Empower vendors to rank all editions of a given product for purposes of determining which subscription changes are upgrades and which are downgrades. Toggle one-click access to free trial editions, with or without prompting the end-user to agree to terms and conditions. Turn vendor access On or Off to configure edition-level restrictions.
    • Payment Requirements — Require customers to provide billing information for all product editions. Require customers to provide billing information to obtain any free trial product editions. Require customers to provide billing information to obtain any fully discounted product editions. Upgrade all free trial products to a paid edition automatically when the customer’s payment method is on record. Specify, edition-by-edition, if and when payment details must be provided and which free-trial editions are upgraded automatically.
    • Contracts — Allow complex stipulations in subscription contracts. Then, when this option is active:
      • Empower vendors to forbid, per edition, any positive or negative change to the number of billable units.
      • Block certain upgrades and downgrades.
      • Control whether increases to contract duration can affect what is considered a downgrade.
      • Block unit decreases on certain contracts.
    • Discounts — Allow customers to “bank” any unused balance from a fixed-price discount after applying it toward a purchase that does not deplete its full value.

Understand recent financial performance quickly

Navigation path:

Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Home | Dashboard

Your marketplace dashboard shows a key performance indicator (KPI) digest and three gauges that summarize the financial performance of your marketplace per week, month, quarter, or year — sometimes alongside calls to action.

The facts that underlie your dashboard gauges are exportable to CSV.

NOTE: Dashboard calls to action, when present, do not pertain to financial settings or transactions. Instead, they pertain to your open action items on other Marketplace Manager pages. For example, they might prompt you to investigate and resolve stalled provisioning of a consumer’s product access.

Understand dashboard gauges

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Home | Dashboard

Your marketplace dashboard shows three gauges and a KPI digest. Collectively, these summarize marketplace performance (across a time span that you choose) in gross revenue, net revenue, total signups, and total orders.

    • The Revenue gauge is a bar chart that tracks both gross and net revenue.
    • The Signups gauge is a line graph that shows the trend of new user account activations.
    • The Orders gauge is a bar chart that tracks the volume of incoming orders.
