Product management overview

As the marketplace provider, your company has many strategic reasons to manage its product inventory with great care.

The first phase of this process is inventory selection. The products in your catalog should accomplish several interconnected goals.

    • Drive casual traffic.
    • Drive conversions and minimize churn by offering real and lasting value to your target customers.
    • Drive long-term profitability for your company and its marketplace partners — including vendors and resellers — by generating revenue that meets or exceeds your forecasts.

After you choose inventory for your catalog, we recommend that your company look at making products easy to find, by organizing and categorizing them in ways that are clear to your target customers.

You might also find it worthwhile to shape product profile page content at a high level. To do this, you can show or hide certain types of marketing content and user-generated content on them universally or conditionally, and you can specify in some cases which values are available for product vendors to cite.

The next phase is promotional. A well designed discount can drive sales generally and a well designed bundle can increase your average deal size.

Understand product catalog curation

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Products

Catalog management features of Marketplace Manager help you to build your business by ensuring a product inventory that is as compelling as you can possibly make it, presented in its most flattering light. You can:

    • Craft product categories, industries, and attributes to target your most promising customer segments. Such tags are fully searchable.
    • Pick and choose the most exciting products to populate your categories.
    • Run tests against product integrations before they go live.
    • Approve or reject the marketing profiles and pricing plans (editions) that your vendors prepare for their products.
    • Accept or reject incoming publication requests from known developers.
    • Drive demand with carousels that highlight featured products, popular products, and staff picks.
    • Mark upcoming additions to your product catalog as Coming Soon.
    • Mark catalog products as Under Maintenance, as needed.
    • Remove from your inventory any products that don’t perform well.

Layout tools help you to shape the presentation and organization of product listing pages in your catalog.

Moreover, you can expand your developer community how you see fit and add yet more cloud services to your marketplace from the AppDirect network catalog, which features hundreds of fully integrated products.

Understand API integration tests

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Products

Information exchange is essential when vendors sell products through your marketplace. Vendors cannot provision consumer access to a product, for example, without knowing how many seats to reserve and assign, according to what cost basis, across what contract duration, and for which registered users in particular. Meanwhile, your marketplace cannot complete ordinary operations without access to real-time vendor provisioning and uptime data.

These few examples do not encompass every motivation for information sharing, of course, but they do illustrate the operational urgency. Neither your marketplace nor its vendors’ products can function as designed without a stable framework to keep mission-critical information flowing freely in both directions.

What supplies the necessary communications framework to accomplish this data exchange? The public AppDirect developer APIs do. Each registered vendor runs dedicated API endpoint servers that listen for (and respond to) API event messages from your marketplace.

API integration tests confirm that vendor API endpoints are reachable, configured correctly, and operating without error.

Product attribute, category, and industry tagging basics

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Products

Certain tags that you apply to a product in your catalog determine which listing pages include it. Tags also help consumers to search and filter the catalog in ways that surface a relevant product faster than simple browsing does.

    • Attribute tags collect products together according to traits that the products themselves share in common, such as their:
      • type — as in “AppDirect Service” products, for example, or “Domain Provider” products.
      • supported operating systems — generally Linux, Microsoft Windows, Android, or Apple iOS.
      • promotional strategy — in bundles or carousels, maybe, or discounted.
    • Category tags collect products together according to their general purpose, such as office productivity, marketing, analytics, or IT management.
    • Industry tags collect products together according to the industries where their use might be of particular interest, such as aerospace, hospitality management, or financial services.

Consumers can employ such tags in combination when they browse and shop in your marketplace.

So, imagine that you sell a configured bundle of IT management products for aerospace workers, and that you have prepared and applied tags for all three of these contingencies.

A consumer can apply such tags as filters and see information about products that match them in combination — with or without any prior knowledge that such products exist or that your marketplace lists them.

Understand lead generation from referral-only products

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Products

A referral-only product is one whose product marketing profile page leads to a purchase flow outside your marketplace. This external purchase flow does not involve AppDirect.

NOTE: Your AppDirect server must run at least the Listing plan before referral-only integrations are available to you.

Understand product profile content restrictions

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Settings | Marketplace Functionality

You can limit whether product marketing profile pages show any:

    • Support tab
    • social media sharing buttons for Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, and email
    • user-submitted questions
    • user-submitted star ratings, before enough are submitted that they exceed a threshold
    • user-submitted reviews from users who did not order the product
    • reference customer citations
