Glossary

 

Jump to terms that start with... [ A | D | E | I | P | U ]

A

active companies

Marketplace Manager UIs do not represent any company as “active” until the company’s first association user completes activation of his or her user account.

active users

The registered and activated users who have placed at least one order apiece or been assigned at least one product subscription seat apiece. Contrast these with registered users who have never activated their accounts, or activated users who have never ordered anything and never benefited from a seat assignment.

D

default user role

A company-level setting that specifies which user role assignment to grant generically to all users upon their registration, within the context of their association with the company.

E

email domain verification

A process that determines whether all registered user accounts that share an email domain in common should also automatically share a company association in common. For example, compare the companies Example One and Example Two.

    • Example One employees use a range of free webmail accounts through a variety of no-cost providers, including Google, Microsoft, Yahoo, and Apple.
    • Example Two employees all use an email server at mail.two.example.com.

In this scenario, Example Two would most likely have a verified email domain, while Example One would not.

NOTE: We prevent verification of the domains associated with many common webmail providers. However, new webmail providers arise continually all over the world, so our verifications blacklist cannot be considered authoritative.

I

inactive users

Registered user accounts that are not yet activated or in use.

Alternatively, users who are both registered and activated, but who have never ordered anything and never benefited from a seat assignment.

invite and assign

A workflow in which company administrators can pre-assign product seats to prospective users, even before their accounts are activated and in use.

P

pending user invitations

User registration invitations whose recipients have not yet clicked through them.

U

user content

Product-specific material that consumers enter, which sometimes loads on the corresponding product profile page, where active users can see it. Marketplace configuration settings determine whether and which consumers are empowered to enter this content, as well as when and if it is published.

    • questions — Questions addressed to the product vendor, which one of the vendor’s developers might answer publicly
    • ratings — Star ratings that summarize product satisfaction
    • reviews — Textual product reviews
