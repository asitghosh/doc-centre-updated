Work with company details

You can examine the low-level details of one registered company, in isolation from all others.

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Home | Companies > { company }

Before you begin

    • Log in to your marketplace as its administrator.

Procedure

    1. Step 1. Go to Manage > Channel > Marketplace.

      2. Step 2. Click past Home | Companies , and then click the company whose details should be reviewed.

The company details view loads.

      Step 3. ( Optional ) Learn company registration and operation details.

      Access

      Check the company’s access levels

          1. a. Look for checked checkboxes under the Company Access label.

      Active or inactive

      Check the company’s activation status

          1. a. Check the LED-style Active icon.

            It is colored green when active or red when inactive.

      Associated users

      Learn about the company’s associated users

          1. a. Check records in the Users table.

      Invite users to join the company

          1. a. Choose Invite Users from the Manage Company hybrid button.

            2. b. Enter email addresses on the Add Emails tab, and then set user roles for the invitees from their respective Role lists.

            OR
            Use the Choose File form on the Upload Email List tab to find and select an ASCII text file from one of your locally mounted or browsable volumes.

            The text file that you choose must contain fewer than 2,000 lines of text, where each line is a valid email address, and where the total filesize is no more than 2MB.

            c. Click Send Invitations.

      Learn about one of the company’s associated users

      See Work with user details

      Creation date

      Learn when the company was registered

          1. a. Look for the date citation after the Created label.

      Default role

      Learn about the company’s default user role assignment

          1. a. Check options under the Default User Role label.

      Email domain

      Learn about the company’s email domain

          1. a. Check the DNS-resolvable domain citation under the Domain label.

      NOTE: The domain citation is missing when the company’s associated users rely on popular, no-cost webmail.

      Learn if the company’s email domain is verified

          1. a. Look for the LED-style Verified | Unverified Email Domain icon.

            It is colored green when verified or red when unverified.

      Verify the company’s email domain

          1. a. Choose Verify Email from the Manage Company hybrid button.

      Enabled or disabled

      Learn about the company’s enablement status

          1. a. Check the LED-style Enabled | Disabled icon.

            It is colored green when enabled or red when inactive.

      Import external products

       

      Allow the company’s administrators to import products

          1. a. Choose Enable Cloud Management from the Manage Company hybrid button.

      Forbid the company’s administrators to import products

          1. a. Choose Disable Cloud Management from the Manage Company hybrid button.

      Logs

      List all of the company’s log messages for a specific event type

          1. a. Click Show Filters on the Logs table.

            2. b. Choose an event type from the Show list.

      List all of the company’s log messages that match a date range

          1. a. Click Show Filters on the Logs table.

            2. b. Define the start and end of an event-logging date range by entering dates manually in the paired date-entry fields.

            OR
            Click a field’s corresponding calendar icon to expose the date-picker widget for populating that field.

      NOTE: When you enter dates manually, use the calendar date notation syntax that is standard in your locale and which your marketplace is configured to use — either M/D/YY or D/M/YY.

      Search all of the company’s log messages

          1. a. Enter your query in the Search field on the Logs table, and then either press Enter or click the magnifying glass icon.

            When your query matches one or more records, we trim the table to expose only these matches.

      Download a CSV file of the company’s log messages

          1. a. Click Download CSV on the Logs table to save a local copy of the table’s underlying records as you have trimmed them.

      Telephone number

      Learn the company’s telephone number

          1. a. Look for the citation after the Phone label.

      Step 4. ( Optional ) Learn company billing details.

      Invoices

      Check for coampany invoices that match a payment state

          1. a. Click Show Filters on the Invoices table.

            2. b. Choose either Paid , Unpaid , or Carried from the Status list.

      Check for company invoices that match a date range

          1. a. Click Show Filters on the Invoices table.

            2. b. Define the start and end of an invoice date range by entering dates manually in the paired date-entry fields.

            OR
            Click a field’s corresponding calendar icon to expose the date-picker widget for populating that field.

      NOTE: When you enter dates manually, use the calendar date notation syntax that is standard in your locale and which your marketplace is configured to use — either M/D/YY or D/M/YY.

      Search all of the company’s invoices

          1. a. Enter your query in the Search field on the Invoices table, and then either press Enter or click the magnifying glass icon.

            When your query matches one or more records, we trim the table to expose only these matches.

      Learn about one of the company’s invoices

      See Work with invoice details

      Orders

      Learn about the company’s orders per product

          1. a. Click Products above the Orders table.

      Learn about the company’s orders per product edition

          1. a. Click Editions above the Orders table.

      Check for company orders that match a state

          1. a. Click Show Filters on the Orders table.

            2. b. Choose an option from the Status list.

      Check for the company’s billable or no-cost orders

          1. a. Click Show Filters on the Orders table.

            2. b. Choose either Paid or Free from the Cost list.

      Check for company orders that match a date range

          1. a. Click Show Filters on the Orders table.

            2. b. Define the start and end of an order’s date range by entering dates manually in the paired date-entry fields.

            OR
            Click a field’s corresponding calendar icon to expose the date-picker widget for populating that field.

      NOTE: When you enter dates manually, use the calendar date notation syntax that is standard in your locale and which your marketplace is configured to use — either M/D/YY or D/M/YY.

      Search all of the company’s orders

          1. a. Enter your query in the Search field on the Orders table, and then either press Enter or click the magnifying glass icon.

            When your query matches one or more records, we trim the table to expose only these matches.

      Check for company orders that are pending, or are not pending

          1. a. Check the Awaiting Vendor Response checkbox to list pending orders exclusively.

            OR
            Clear the checkbox to hide pending orders.

      Download a CSV file of the company’s filtered order records

          1. a. Click Download CSV on the Orders table to save a local copy of the table’s underlying records as you have trimmed them.

      Learn about one of the company’s orders

      See Work with order details

      Payments

      Check for company payments with a specified outcome

          1. a. Click Show Filters on the Payments table.

            2. b. Choose an option from the Result list.

      Check for company payments that match a date range

          1. a. Define the start and end of an payment’s date range by entering dates manually in the paired date-entry fields.

            OR
            Click a field’s corresponding calendar icon to expose the date-picker widget for populating that field.

      NOTE: When you enter dates manually, use the calendar date notation syntax that is standard in your locale and which your marketplace is configured to use — either M/D/YY or D/M/YY.

      Search all of the company’s payments

          1. a. Enter your query in the Search field on the Payments table, and then either press Enter or click the magnifying glass icon.

            When your query matches one or more records, we trim the table to expose only these matches.

      Learn about one of the company’s payments

      See Work with payment details

      Product contracts

      Check for any contracts without billing

          1. a. Look for the citation under the Free Trials label.

      Check for any expired contracts without billing

          1. a. Look for the citation under the Expired Free Trials label.

      Check for any billable contracts

          1. a. Look for the citation under the Purchased Products label.

      Check for any suspended contracts

          1. a. Look for the citation under the Suspended Products label.

      Total spent

      Check the company’s total spending

          1. a. Look for the citation under the Total Spent label.

      Step 5. Stop. You have completed this procedure.
