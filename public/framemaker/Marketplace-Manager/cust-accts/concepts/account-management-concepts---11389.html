Understand the sitewide company directory

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Home | Companies

When you filter, search, and sort registered companies by one or more of their attributes, you can discover important details about them very quickly. For example, you can learn any of the following.

    • when any company was first activated on your marketplace
    • the office telephone number for any company
    • how many registered users are associated with a company
    • whether a company is registered as a vendor for your marketplace
    • whether anyone at a company has channel administrator privileges in your marketplace

Clicking through a record in the returned list loads a more detailed record of the described company.

Clicking the CSV icon exports the refined list to comma-separated values. You can then import the CSV data into any database or spreadsheet for manipulation and analysis.

Understand a company details page

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Home | Companies > { company }

You can click through any listed record on the Companies page. When you do, Marketplace Manager shows details about the corresponding company. These details communicate all of the following.

    • when a record of the company was first created
    • whether the company, although registered, is also activationd on your marketplace
    • which email domain your marketplace associates with the company
    • whether the company’s email domain is verification
    • what tax rate applies in the company’s physical locale
    • whether the company is registered as a vendor for your marketplace
    • whether the company’s administrators are empowered to assign certain, reserved user roles
    • which user role type is granted by default to the company’s associated users
    • exactly how much money the company has spent thus far through your marketplace
    • real-time counts of all the company’s:
      • free trials
      • expired free trials
      • purchased products
      • suspended products
      • unpaid invoices

Tables on each company details page help you to search, filter, and track records of all individual user accounts, orders, invoices, payments, and server log entries that are associated with the described company.

Furthermore, a set of company management options help you to enable or disable the company and verify its email domain. And, when you are not already associated with the described company, you can, at your discretion, join it.

Understand the sitewide user directory

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Home | Users

When you filter, search, and sort user account records by one or more of their attributes, you can discover important details about them very quickly. For example, you can learn any of the following.

    • company associations
    • names
    • email domains
    • account creation dates
    • account activation states
    • customer IDs

Then:

    • Clicking through a record in the returned list loads a more detailed record of the described user.
    • Clicking the CSV icon exports the refined list to comma-separated values.

Understand a user details page

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Home | Users > { user }

You can click through any listed record on the Users page. When you do, Marketplace Manager shows details about the corresponding user account. These details communicate many things.

    • when a record of the user account was first created
    • whether the user’s account, though registered, has also been activated on your marketplace
    • which email address — or addresses — your marketplace associates with the user
    • whether the user has any administrative role at his or her associated company
    • exactly how much money the user has spent thus far through your marketplace
    • real-time counts of all the user’s:
      • free trials
      • expired free trials
      • purchased products
      • suspended products
      • unpaid invoices

Tables on each user details page help you to search, filter, and track records of all orders, invoices, payments, and server log entries that are associated with the described user account.

Furthermore, a set of user management options help you to do many things.

    • enable or disable the user account
    • send the described user a password reset message through email.
    • request revocable 24-hour impersonation privileges to deliver technical support
    • impersonate the described user, when authorized, for any portion of the 24-hour approval period
    • make purchases on behalf of the described user
    • toggle between and among all of the described user’s associated companies

And, when you are not already associated with one of the described user’s associated companies, you have the option to join it.
