Account management overview

The AppDirect database organizes a set of records that describe certain entities individually and collectively, while also tracking their interactions and dependencies. These entity types include companies and users. Companies and users are the only entity types that can enter into contractual relationships, have debt, make payments against debt, or receive payments. All marketplace activity and revenue depends on them.

Companies and user accounts are bound together

    • Each registered user is at least one company’s constituent.
    • Some users maintain simultaneous associations with multiple companies.
    • Each company gives context to at least one registered user.
    • Most companies have multiple associated users.

No user account can exist outside its necessary context. At a minimum, high-level metadata for a given company must be saved in database records before the records that describe any of the company’s constituent user accounts can be saved.

Similarly, a company can exist only as long as it has at least one associated user account.

NOTE: In the logic that underlies our database schema, a ‘company’ is an organization, not necessarily a business. Its mission could differ from the mission that a business pursues. Such differences are not relevant for account management purposes. We mean only to label the entity type that centralizes billing and user management for a set of related user accounts.

The ‘first user’ designation is not an assignable user role

Because companies and user accounts are bound together, the workflow to create a company includes mandatory designation of the company’s first associated user account. In most cases, this user account belongs to someone who should have the company administrator role.

Users can be invited, activated, synchronized, and migrated

Typically, a company administrator either invites his or her coworkers to activation associations to their company or syncs user accounts with their company’s Microsoft Active Directory server.

NOTE: Activation applies to companies as well as users. A company is not considered active until its “first user” activates his or her user account.

A reseller can create a company and populate it by inviting potential users to join.

A marketplace provider can migrate imported company and user records in bulk from a legacy cloud service commerce platform.

Authentication separates users from visitors

Some marketplaces display their product discovery features even to people who are not logged in (authenticated). We call such people visitors. Other marketplaces display nothing but signup and login forms to their visitors.

    • There is no assignable user role that makes someone a visitor.
    • Many visitors are not registered users. They have no defined authority or access. They see only what a marketplace does not hide.
    • Other visitors have registered user accounts but are not logged in.

Visitors who click any Try Now or Buy Now button on a product marketing profile page cannot enter the product’s purchase flow. Instead, we prompt them immediately to either log in to their registered user account — supposing that they might have one without being logged in to it currently — or register as a new user.

Role assignments often expand user authority

A user role is a defined authorization scope. Most role assignments are additive, expanding a registered user’s authority to see and use various features.

NOTE: Although most role assignments are additive, some subtract from what a user can see. For example, a user who has the reseller role can see UI elements in Reseller Center that are hidden from someone whose role assignments combine the reseller role and the channel administrator role.

Low-access users

Every registered user has at least one assigned user role. However, the least powerful role — the ordinary consumer that we call an end-user — is assigned implicitly to all registered users automatically. At a minimum, end-users can receive product assignments and use their respective MyApps pages.

TIP: All other assignable roles can also access these features.

It is possible but rare to assign this role, except when reassigning it to someone whose end-user role assignment you revoked previously.

User role assignment options are situational

Any or all of the following variables can limit which roles are available for assignment to a given user.

    • which of the target user’s company associations is selected during role assignment, supposing that the user is associated with more than one company
    • which plan the marketplace provider purchased, such as “listing” or “network”
    • which optional services the marketplace provider purchased, such as “reseller management”
    • how the marketplace is configured and customized
    • which user roles are applied to the role-assigner’s own account

User groups simplify user account management

Company administrators can organize their company’s associated users into user group, and then manage user activities and product assignments collectively. These logical groupings can take any form, including worker locale, job title, department, manager, or time zone.

As a channel administrator , you benefit from the implicit user grouping that occurs on a company-by-company basis through company-user associations. Your actions that affect a company affect all of its associated users.

Your marketplace supports multiple user authentication models

Your AppDirect marketplace is both a provider and a consumer of authentication services.

    • Registered companies can consume OpenID authentication services provided through Google or other external providers.
    • Registered companies can consume Microsoft Active Directory authentication services that their own servers provide, for user base synchronization.
    • After any registered users receive seat assignments for products that your marketplace lists, your marketplace becomes the identity provider for their SSO access to these products. Clicking any product tile on a MyApps page initiates an SSO session with the product’s vendor.
    • In addition, company administrators can turn two-factor authentication On or Off for their associated users.
