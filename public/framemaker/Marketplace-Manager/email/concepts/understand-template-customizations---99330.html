Understand template customizations

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Custom UI | Customer Emails

You control and personalize the content in every sent message, through your use of a given template’s supported parameters. Furthermore, you can enable or disable any optional template, configure certain templates to send message copies automatically to a sales agent, and populate the “unsubscribe” text that certain templates inject into their generated messages.

Understand the ‘Unsubscribe Message’ template

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Custom UI | Customer Emails

Several notification message templates inject into their generated messages a short text string that they all share in common, which you can either edit in isolation from the templates that call upon it, or never edit. Until and unless you edit it, the text string is always, literally, the word “Unsubscribe.”

This text is the clickable, human-readable representation of a lengthy, machine-generated link in the message footer, for templates whose recipients can unsubscribe from their future output.

The logic underlying which parameters a template supports

Essentially all templates support a set of six baseline parameters, regardless of the message recipient or context. These parameters cite only the most fundamental marketplace attributes.

    • {BASE_URL}
    • {PARTNER_LABEL}
    • {PARTNER_STORE_LABEL}
    • {SUPPORT_EMAIL}
    • {SUPPORT_PHONE}
    • {SUPPORT_URL}

Additional parameters come into play when company administrators invite unregistered users to join their company on your marketplace, or when a sales agent — meaning, someone with either the reseller or the channel sales support user role — adds unregistered users to a company. These message parameters pertain to the invitation itself, before it is accepted and before the invited user has activated his or her newly registered account.

    • ACTIVATION_URL
    • ADMIN_NAME
    • INVITATION_URL
    • LOGIN_URL
    • SALES_AGENT_EMAIL
    • SALES_AGENT_NAME

Most templates also support a third set of parameters, which become relevant only after a registered user has activated his or her account on your marketplace and is associated with a registered company. This third set of parameters pertains to the message recipient and whichever one of his or her associated companies is relevant in the context that triggers a generated message.

TIP: Understand that a given user account can be associated with more than one company at a time — because one company association per user is the enforced minimum. There is no enforced maximum number of company associations per user.

    • Either {COMPANY_EXTERNAL_ID} or {COMPANY_UUID}, depending on how your marketplace is configured to identify its member companies
    • {COMPANY_NAME}
    • {USER_CITY}
    • {USER_COUNTRY}
    • {USER_EMAIL}
    • Either {USER_EXTERNAL_ID} or {USER_UUID}, depending on how your marketplace is configured to identify its registered users
    • {USER_FIRST_NAME}
    • {USER_FULL_NAME}
    • {USER_LANGUAGE}
    • {USER_LAST_NAME}
    • {USER_POSTAL_CODE}
    • {USER_STATE}
    • {USER_STREET}

A fourth set of parameters becomes available when the default language on your marketplace uses a gendered grammar, which enforces levels of formality that languages with a non-gendered grammar do not enforce.

    • {SALUTATION}
    • {USER_TITLE}

Finally, individual templates can support one or more parameters beyond those in the predetermined sets, which are specific to each template’s unique context and purpose.

Inventory of templates by message recipient

Tables in this section expose which templates are associated with each message recipient enumerator type.

channel admin

Messages target someone with the channel administrator user role. See Templates for the ‘channel admin’ enumerator.

company admin

Messages target Someone with the company administrator user role, whose company — or one of its associated users, ordered products, or payment methods — is the message topic. See Templates for the ‘company admin’ enumerator.

customer support

Messages target someone with the channel customer support user role. See Templates for the ‘customer support’ enumerator.

existing user

Messages target someone with any or no assigned user role, whose account on your marketplace has been activated. See Templates for the ‘existing user’ enumerator.

new user

Messages target someone with any or no assigned user role, for whom a user record exists, but whose account on your marketplace is not yet activated. See Templates for the ‘new user’ enumerator.

product developer

Messages target someone with the developer user role, whose product is the message topic. See Templates for the ‘product developer’ enumerator.

product owner

Messages target someone with any assigned user role, who is held accountable for complying with a contract’s end-user terms and conditions. Typically, this is either the user who created the relevant order or someone with the billing administrator user role, who has taken over responsibility for billing and contract compliance. See Templates for the ‘product owner’ enumerator.

product user

Messages target someone with any assigned user role, who has also been assigned to consume one of the described product’s allocated seats. See Templates for the ‘product user’ enumerator.

reseller

Messages target someone with the reseller user role. See Templates for the ‘reseller’ enumerator.

reseller manager

Messages target someone with the reseller manager user role. See Templates for the ‘reseller manager’ enumerator.
